Schedule

Please note that this schedule is tentative and may change at any point in time leading up to the event.

Thursday December 5th, 2013

Opening Reception at Falcon's Nest :: 7:00PM - 10:00PM

After a long day of travel, join us and your fellow attendees at the local sports bar, The Falcon's Nest. Full of incredible aviation memorabilia, billiard tables, and TVs, the Falcon's Nest has something for everyone - even if just sitting out on the deck strikes your fancy. We will have appetizers available for you to munch on. It is a quick (~2 minute) walk from the hotel and we look forward to seeing you there!

Friday December 6th, 2013

Registration, Breakfast, and Coffee/Tea :: 8:00 AM - 10:00AM

Meet us in front of the Amelia Ballroom (our main session room in the conference center) for event registration. Grab your bag and badge then head on over for breakfast, coffee/tea, and some great discussions.

Opening Remarks and Introductions from the RobotsConf Team :: 9:00AM - 9:30AM

Conference introductions and a quick overview of how RobotsConf will work (for those of you that aren't reading this schedule). We will introduce you to the staff and the experts we have assembled at the event to help you build amazing things throughout the next 36 hours. These individuals will be your guides on the journey towards hardware enlightenment, take full advantage of the time you have with them!

Visionary Lectures and Leaders :: 9:30AM - 11:30AM

What is a conference without a couple talks that show you the world from a different perspective? RobotsConf will kick off with a series of high-impact, visionary lectures from some of the greatest thought leaders of the Maker Movement. Individuals will present not just ideas, but projects and technologies that they have constructed. These talks will provide the frame of reference for the rest of the day and hopefully the rest of your maker career.

Lunch :: 11:30AM - 12:45PM

Food is the fuel that will drive you through the next couple hours. Fill up on food and conversations about hardware hacking and robots before we enter into the workshop component of the event. The lunch will have vegan-friendly options and strict vegan meals available upon request.

Small Group Breakout :: 12:45PM - 1:00PM

The remainder of the scheduled events for the day will be done in small groups working through specific workshops. Small groups will be broken into and presented with their specific schedule of workshop times. Groups will move from workshop to workshop in order to quickly get exposure and insight into the specialized topics at each workshop. The groups will be assigned randomly in order to encourage you to meet new attendees at RobotsConf, possibly even with people you may want to build your robots.

Workshops :: 1:00PM - 6:00PM

Around the great room of RobotsConf we will have specialized workshop stations set up for the following topics.

  • Station 1: 3D Printing and Modern Fabrication
  • Station 2: Drones and Autonoumous/Semi-Autonomous Vehicles
  • Station 3: Microcontrollers and the Internet of Things
  • Station 4: Home Automation
  • Station 5: Electronic Fundamentals
  • Station 6: Soldering, Wiring, and Prototyping
  • Station 7: Robotics and ROS
  • Station 8: HackerSpaces
  • Station 9: Interaction Interfaces (Makey-Makey, Kinect, LeapMotion)
  • Station 10: Fashion

They are designed to provide you with a quick, hands-on, rapid fire overview of each of the major segments within the Maker movement. Your group will round robin through all of the staions such that by 6:00PM, you should be knowledgeable about all of the areas and hopefully you will have identified a small subset that deeply interest or excite you. The goal of these workshops isn't necessarily to explain everything in 30 minutes (an impossible task) but to give you enough insight to know if you need to return to the workshop later to accomplish your robotic goals! Think of it as speed dating for the maker movement.

Open Hack :: 6:00PM - 1:00AM

If you have an idea and a team, you can start breadboarding and soldering items together to build your invention, otherwise use this time to form a team (or go solo, that is perfectly fine!) and come up with what you are going to build. For those so inclined, we will be hosting the first of several sumobot tournaments starting at 10:30PM with bots controlled by our local high-level language experts. The tournament is a great way to start down a path of building something that you can then compete and evolve over time. For an example of what a sumobot tournament will look like, see below:

During the open hack, you will have you have access to the best possible people in your preferred language there to help (and independence for when you don't) as well as all of the workshop experts. There will be drones available to hack on, solder stations ready to melt metal, and more servos than you can possibly imagine. The evening will buzz with the excitement and energy that only comes when you are building your very own electronic inventions.

Evening Social Event :: 8:00PM - 11:00PM

RobotsConf is not just about soldering components together, but also about bringing people together. As such, we want to provide you with a great evening social event, while still allowing you to participate in the Open Hack night (or just discuss and watch). Right in the same area as the open hack event, we will be hosting one of our amazing social events so if you need a break from hardware hacking, you can do that or just grab a quick refreshment and get back at it!

Saturday December 7th, 2013

Open Hack :: 9:00AM - 2:00PM

Continue to hack forward after a refreshing bit of sleep (or pool time) as you race to the finish line and show and tell. Throughout the day, we will have sumobot breaks as the brackets continue to narrow down.

RobotsConf Show and Tell :: 2:00PM - 3:30PM
